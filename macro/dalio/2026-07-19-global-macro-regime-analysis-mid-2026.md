# Global Macro Regime Analysis — Mid-2026

## Executive Summary

The global macroeconomic landscape in mid-2026 is defined by **deep bifurcation** between the two largest economies. China is trapped in a "stagnation-lite" regime with structural credit contraction and collapsing fixed investment, while simultaneously experiencing a producer-price reflation shock. The US remains in a late-cycle expansion with solid growth, full employment, and yields elevated above 4.5%. The net result is a fragmented global regime with no clear directional signal — a scenario that demands differentiated regional positioning.

---

## 1. China: Stagnation-Lite + Producer Reflation

### 1.1 Growth: Below Target, Structurally Weak

**Key observations:**

- GDP decelerated from 5.0% (Q1) to 4.7% (H1 average), suggesting Q2 was ~4.4%. The trajectory is **clearly weakening**.
- **Retail sales at 1.0%** is the smoking gun for the consumer-led demand problem. Households are not spending.
- **FAI at -15.6%** signals a full-blown investment depression, driven overwhelmingly by the real estate sector contraction.
- Exports surging at 27% is the **only bright spot** — but this is external demand, not domestic, and may be vulnerable to trade headwinds.
- PMIs hovering at the 50 line indicate the manufacturing sector is stagnating, not growing.

**Growth verdict:** Below-target, domestic-demand-deprived, export-supported. **Stagnation-lite.**

### 1.2 Inflation: CPI Benign, PPI Spiking (Key Risk)

| Indicator | Value | Period | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| CPI yoy | 1.0% | June 2026 | Benign |
| CPI yoy | 1.2% | May 2026 | Benign |
| PPI yoy | **4.1%** | June 2026 | **Rising rapidly** |
| PPI yoy | 3.9% | May 2026 | Rising |
| PPI yoy | 2.8% | April 2026 | Rising |
| PPI yoy | 0.5% | March 2026 | Inflection |
| PPI yoy | -0.9% | Feb 2026 | Deflation |
| CGPI Agricultural | -4.9% | June 2026 | Deflation |
| CGPI Energy | **17.7%** | June 2026 | **Surge** |

**Key observations:**

- CPI is stable at 1.0-1.3% — no consumer inflation problem. This is consistent with weak domestic demand.
- **PPI has inflected sharply**, moving from -0.9% (Feb) to +4.1% (June) in just four months — a **5 percentage point swing**.
- The PPI surge is **supply-side driven** by energy costs: CGPI Energy at +17.7%. This is a global commodity/energy shock transmitting to Chinese producers.
- CGPI Agricultural at -4.9% deflation shows the farm sector is in its own demand slump — consistent with weak rural consumption.
- The **PPI-CPI divergence** is widening, squeezing corporate margins: producers face rising input costs but cannot pass through to consumers due to weak demand.

**Inflation verdict:** CPI is not a concern. The **real story is PPI reflation** — a cost-push phenomenon that will compress industrial profits unless demand recovers.

| Aggregate Financing | 6245.0 | April 2026 | Modest |

**Key observations:**

- **Monetary policy is extremely loose** — LPR at 3.0%, SHIBOR 3M at 1.43%, 10Y bonds yielding only 1.74%. This is crisis-level accommodation.
- **But credit is contracting: new loans are down 25.2% yoy.** This is the classic "pushing on a string" problem. The banks are willing to lend, but the private sector (especially real estate and households) does not want to borrow.
- M2 at 8% is moderate and stable — no money printing panic.
- The yield curve is positively sloped (0.48%) but mostly because the short end is at absurdly low levels.
- USD/CNY at 6.78 is relatively stable, suggesting FX policy is holding the line despite economic weakness.

**Policy verdict:** Policy is maximally accommodative but **transmission is broken**. The credit channel is blocked by real estate distress and weak consumer confidence.

### 1.4 Real Estate: The Core Problem

- **FAI: -15.6% yoy** — Fixed asset investment collapse is almost entirely real-estate-driven.
- **New Loans: -25.2%** — Credit flowing into the economy is drying up.
- **House Price Index: +0.5%** — Prices are barely stable; this likely masks significant regional divergence.
- **Consumer Confidence: 89.9** — Depressed confidence feeds the property market malaise in a vicious cycle.

The property sector remains the **central drag** on the Chinese economy. Until housing stabilizes, domestic demand will remain suppressed.

**US verdict:** The US is in a classic **late-cycle expansion** — growth is solid, labor is tight, and inflation is not fully defeated. The Fed will remain cautious about cutting rates.

## 4. Key Risks & Implications

### 4.1 China Risks

1. **PPI-to-CPI pass-through**: If energy/commodity costs eventually push through to consumer prices, China could face **"stagflation-lite"** — weak growth + rising CPI. This would severely constrain PBOC's willingness to ease further.

2. **Real estate contagion deepening**: FAI -15.6% suggests the property depression is still accelerating, not stabilizing. Developer debt risks remain systemic.

3. **Export vulnerability**: With exports surging at 27%, a softening in global demand or escalation in trade tensions would remove the only growth engine.

4. **Credit event risk**: New loans -25.2% with ultra-low rates suggests a banking system that is willing but unable to lend. Non-performing loans may be rising beneath the surface.

### 4.2 US Risks

1. **Inflation reacceleration**: If core CPI stays at ~0.3% m/m, the Fed will have no room to cut, keeping financial conditions tight.

2. **Growth slowdown**: Late-cycle expansions can end abruptly. If the labor market softens, the US could shift from expansion to contraction quickly.

3. **Fiscal sustainability**: With yields at 4.57%, US fiscal dynamics are under pressure — a risk for longer-duration assets.

### 4.3 Cross-Border Implications

1. **China-US yield differential**: China 10Y at 1.74% vs US 10Y at 4.57% = **283bp gap**. This exerts continuous depreciation pressure on CNY unless capital controls hold.

2. **Commodity channel**: China's PPI surge (partly driven by global energy prices) means Chinese demand for commodities is not the problem — it's global supply / energy geopolitics.

3. **Portfolio implications**: The China-US growth differential has flipped — the US is now growing faster than China. This is unusual and has implications for capital flows (favoring US assets).

## 5. Investment Conclusions

1. **China fixed income**: With 10Y at 1.74%, bonds are pricing in continued weakness. But the PPI reflation risk is not priced. **Rates may have a floor here** — further significant declines are unlikely.

2. **China equities**: The macro backdrop (weak demand, margin squeeze from PPI, credit contraction) is not supportive for broad equity exposure. Selective opportunities exist in exporters and energy-related sectors.

3. **US Treasuries**: At 4.57%, 10Y yields are attractive for carry, but the inflation stickiness risk means there's no imminent catalyst for a rally. Patience required.

4. **USD/CNY**: The 283bp yield differential favors USD. Expect continued managed depreciation of CNY toward the 6.8-7.0 range.

5. **Commodities**: China's PPI reflation (CGPI Energy +17.7%) suggests energy and industrial commodities remain supported by supply constraints, not demand.

6. **Global risk assets**: The bifurcated regime favors an **un-correlated, regional approach** rather than a directional global bet. Overweight US, underweight China-exposed EM, neutral Europe/Japan.
