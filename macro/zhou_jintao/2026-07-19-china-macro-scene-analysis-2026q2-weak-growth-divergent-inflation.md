# China Macro Scene Analysis — 2026 Q2

**Analysis Date**: 2026-07-18
**Data Freshness**: CPI/PPI/PMI/FAI/Retail Sales as of June 2026; GDP as of H1 2026; Bond yields as of 2026-07-17

---

## 1. Growth Assessment: WEAK / DECELERATING

| Indicator | Value | Period | Assessment |
|-----------|-------|--------|------------|
| GDP YoY | 4.7% | H1 2026 | Below 5% official target, clear deceleration |
| Manufacturing PMI | 50.3 | June 2026 | Barely above 50, borderline expansion |
| Non-manufacturing PMI | 50.2 | June 2026 | Same, services barely expanding |
| Retail Sales YoY | +1.0% | June 2026 | **Extremely weak consumption** |
| Fixed Asset Investment YoY | **-15.6%** | June 2026 | **Severe contraction — historic low** |
| Consumer Confidence | 89.9 | May 2026 | Below neutral (100), weak sentiment |
| New Loans YoY | **-25.2%** | June 2026 | **Credit demand collapsing** |

**Growth Verdict**: The growth engine is clearly stalling. GDP running 0.3ppt below target. The most alarming signals are:
- **FAI at -15.6%**: This is a collapse in investment activity, likely driven by property sector distress, local government financing constraints, and private sector pessimism.
- **Retail sales at +1.0%**: Consumption is barely growing in nominal terms — real consumption is likely negative after accounting for CPI.
- **New loans -25.2%**: The credit channel is frozen. Businesses and households are not borrowing, despite low rates.

## 2. Inflation Assessment: DIVERGENT (Low CPI + Rising PPI)

| Indicator | Value | Period | Assessment |
|-----------|-------|--------|------------|
| CPI YoY | +1.0% | June 2026 | **Low, benign** — well below 3% target |
| CPI MoM | -0.3% | June 2026 | Deflationary month-on-month |
| PPI YoY | **+4.1%** | June 2026 | **Accelerating rapidly** from -0.9% in Feb |
| CGPI Energy YoY | **+17.7%** | June 2026 | **Sharp energy cost inflation** |
| CGPI Agriculture YoY | -4.9% | June 2026 | Agricultural deflation |

**Inflation Verdict**: The inflation picture is **structurally divergent**:

- **Consumer side (CPI 1.0%)**: Weak demand is keeping consumer prices subdued. The 0.3% MoM decline in June suggests deflationary pressure at the consumer level. This is consistent with weak retail sales and low consumer confidence.

- **Producer side (PPI 4.1%)**: PPI has staged a remarkable turnaround from -0.9% in February 2026 to +4.1% in June 2026 — a 5ppt swing in 4 months. The main driver appears to be energy costs (CGPI Energy +17.7%). This represents **cost-push inflation** hitting producers.

- **Key Risk**: If PPI continues to accelerate while CPI remains low, corporate profit margins will be severely squeezed (producers can't pass through costs to consumers in a weak demand environment). This is the classic "profit squeeze" scenario.

## 3. Policy Assessment: ACCOMMODATIVE but TRANSMISSION-IMPAIRED

| Indicator | Value | Period | Assessment |
|-----------|-------|--------|------------|
| LPR 1Y | 3.0% | Current | Low rate — accommodative |
| SHIBOR 3M | 1.43% | 2026-07-17 | Very low — abundant liquidity |
| Bond 10Y Yield | 1.74% | 2026-07-17 | Low — bond market pricing in weak growth |
| Bond 2Y Yield | 1.26% | 2026-07-17 | Very low — policy rate expectations |
| 10Y-2Y Spread | 0.48% | 2026-07-17 | Steepening — reflects growth pessimism |

**Policy Verdict**: 
- **Monetary policy is clearly accommodative**: LPR at 3.0%, SHIBOR at 1.43%, and the yield curve is steep. The PBOC is providing ample liquidity.
- **BUT transmission is broken**: Despite low rates, new loans are down 25.2% YoY and FAI is collapsing at -15.6%. This is the classic **"pushing on a string"** problem — you can lead a horse to water but you can't make it drink.
- **Fiscal policy implications**: The data suggests monetary easing alone is insufficient. Significant fiscal stimulus (direct government spending, not just credit easing) would be needed to break the cycle.

## 4. Scene Classification

### Primary Regime: **Weak Growth + Divergent Inflation (Low CPI / Surging PPI) + Accommodative but Blocked Policy Transmission**

| Dimension | Classification | Confidence |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| **Growth** | Weak / Decelerating | 90% |
| **Consumer Inflation** | Below-target / Benign | 85% |
| **Producer Inflation** | Accelerating / Cost-push emerging | 80% |
| **Monetary Policy** | Accommodative | 90% |
| **Credit Transmission** | Impaired / Frozen | 85% |
| **Overall Regime** | **Stagflationary tilt (weak growth + cost-push PPI + broken credit channel)** | 85% |

### Secondary Risks:
1. **Balance sheet recession dynamics**: The combination of collapsing FAI, frozen credit, and weak consumption is consistent with private sector deleveraging — a classic balance sheet recession pattern.
2. **Profit margin compression**: PPI-CPI spread widening (currently ~3.1ppt) squeezes corporate profitability, especially for downstream manufacturers.
3. **Policy impotence risk**: If credit demand continues to collapse despite record-low rates, the PBOC may need to resort to unconventional measures (helicopter money, direct fiscal monetization).

## 5. Key Monitoring Points

1. **PPI trajectory**: If PPI breaks above 5%, the cost-push narrative becomes more concerning. Monitor energy CGPI for leading signals.
2. **FAI and new loans**: Any stabilization in these two metrics would be the first sign that the credit freeze is thawing.
3. **Fiscal policy response**: Given that monetary transmission is broken, watch for signs of direct fiscal stimulus (infrastructure bonds, consumption subsidies, etc.).
4. **CPI dynamics**: If CPI starts to follow PPI higher (lagged pass-through), the PBOC's room for further easing would narrow.
