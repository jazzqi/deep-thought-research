# Global Macro Regime Assessment — July 2026

## Executive Summary

The global macroeconomic landscape in mid-2026 is defined by a **stark policy-and-cycle divergence** between the United States and China. The US economy continues to exhibit solid growth with sticky core inflation, supporting a "higher for longer" rate environment. China, by contrast, is trapped in a **demand-deficient, quasi-deflationary slowdown** where aggressive monetary easing fails to translate into credit and investment demand. This asymmetry creates a challenging environment for global asset allocation.

**Interpretation:** China's growth engine is sputtering. The GDP print of 4.7% falls short of the 5% target. The **investment channel has broken down** — FAI contracting 15.6% YoY and new loans down 25.2% signal that neither the property sector nor infrastructure is absorbing credit. Retail sales growth of just 1.0% indicates households are not spending despite low rates. PMIs hovering barely above 50 confirm manufacturing and services are in a near-stall speed. **Export strength (27% YoY)** and **import strength (36% YoY)** are bright spots, likely driven by AI/semiconductor supply chain demand and commodity imports, but these are insufficient to offset the domestic demand vacuum.

## 2. Inflation

### China: Near-Deflation with Cost-Push Undercurrents

| Indicator | Value | Period | Assessment |
|-----------|-------|--------|------------|
| CPI (YoY) | **1.0%** | Jun 2026 | **Very low, near-deflation** |
| PPI (YoY) | **4.1%** | Jun 2026 | Moderate producer inflation |
| CGPI - Agricultural (YoY) | **-4.9%** | Jun 2026 | Deflation in farm goods |
| CGPI - Energy (YoY) | **17.7%** | Jun 2026 | Sharp energy cost increase |

**Interpretation:** China's CPI at 1.0% is well below the PBOC's 3% target and approaching deflationary territory. The collapse in agricultural commodity prices (-4.9%) is dragging food prices down, while weak consumer demand prevents pass-through of any cost pressures. At the producer level, PPI at 4.1% is driven entirely by the **energy commodity spike (+17.7%)**, likely reflecting geopolitical premia and supply constraints. However, the agricultural-to-energy divergence means the economy faces **asymmetric cost pressures** — energy costs squeeze industrial margins while farm deflation benefits food consumers but hurts rural incomes. The net effect is a disinflationary demand environment with pockets of supply-side cost pressure.

## 3. Policy Regime

### China: Accommodative Policy with Transmission Failure

| Indicator | Value | Period | Assessment |
|-----------|-------|--------|------------|
| LPR 1Y | **3.0%** | Current | Low, accommodative |
| SHIBOR 3M | **1.43%** | Jul 2026 | Very low interbank rates |
| 10Y Bond Yield | **1.74%** | Jul 2026 | **Historic low** |
| 2Y Bond Yield | **1.27%** | Jul 2026 | Extremely low |
| 10Y-2Y Spread | **0.48%** | Jul 2026 | Positive but flat |
| M2 Growth (YoY) | **8.0%** | Jun 2026 | Moderate money supply |
| Aggregate Financing | **6,245** | Apr 2026 | Mixed signal |

**Interpretation:** China's bond market is screaming deflation. The 10Y yield at 1.74% is at historic lows, reflecting expectations of **prolonged economic weakness and potential further easing**. The yield curve is positively sloped (48bps), but given the level, this is a "low-for-long" signal rather than a growth optimism signal. SHIBOR at 1.43% and LPR at 3.0% confirm that the PBOC has thrown the kitchen sink at monetary easing.

**However, the transmission mechanism is broken.** Despite M2 growing at 8%, new loans are collapsing 25.2% YoY and FAI is dropping 15.6%. This is the textbook definition of a **"pushing on a string"** scenario — banks have liquidity, but borrowers (households, developers, local government financing vehicles) lack the credit demand or creditworthiness to absorb it. The consumer confidence reading of 89.9 confirms households are in a precautionary savings mode.

### United States: Restrictive-to-Neutral, "Higher for Longer"

| Indicator | Value | Period | Assessment |
|-----------|-------|--------|------------|
| 10Y Treasury Yield | **4.57%** | Jul 2026 | Elevated vs. recent history |
| 2Y Treasury Yield | **4.16%** | Jul 2026 | Well above neutral estimate |
| 2s10s Spread | **+0.41%** | Jul 2026 | **Curve normalized** |

**Interpretation:** The US yield curve has normalized after a prolonged inversion — the 2s10s spread is now +41bps. Historically, curve normalization signals either (a) recession fears have fully receded (soft landing achieved), or (b) the market is pricing in eventual rate cuts on the short end. Given GDP at 3.3% and core CPI still sticky, the **"no landing" or "soft landing" narrative** is the dominant interpretation. The Fed remains in restrictive territory with the effective fed funds rate likely at 5.25-5.50%, and the bond market sees **no urgency for cuts** in the near term.

## 4. Cross-Cutting Observations

### China-US Divergence

The two largest economies are in entirely different cycles:

| Dimension | China | US |
|-----------|-------|----|
| Growth Momentum | Below target, decelerating | Above trend, resilient |
| Inflation | Near-deflation (CPI 1.0%) | Sticky (Core MoM 0.3%) |
| Policy Stance | Aggressively accommodative | Restrictive/Neutral |
| Credit Cycle | Collapsing demand (-25% loans) | Still functioning |
| Bond Market Signal | Deflation pricing (1.74%) | Sticky inflation (4.57%) |
| Currency | USD/CNY ~6.78, managed | Strong USD |

### Notable Risk Factors

1. **China's investment collapse is structural.** FAI at -15.6% is not just a property story — it reflects a generalized de-leveraging across the economy. Until household and business confidence recovers, monetary easing alone will not work. Fiscal stimulus or structural reforms are needed.

2. **Energy-driven PPI in China.** CGPI Energy at +17.7%, combined with PPI at 4.1%, suggests Chinese industrial margins are being squeezed by commodity costs while final demand is too weak to pass through. This is a profit squeeze for the manufacturing sector.

3. **US commercial bank deposits dropping** (-$74B to $19.36T per recent news) bears watching. While not crisis-level, it suggests liquidity is gradually leaving the banking system, potentially tightening financial conditions further even if the Fed holds rates.

4. **USD/CNY stability at 6.78** is notable given China's weak fundamentals. This likely reflects active FX management (PBOC defense) rather than market equilibrium. A potential risk is a managed depreciation if growth continues to deteriorate.

## 6. Investment Implications

1. **China bonds:** The 1.74% 10Y yield may still have room to fall further if growth disappoints. Duration play remains valid but crowded.
2. **China equities:** Domestic demand proxies (retail, consumer, property) remain challenged. Export-oriented and AI/semiconductor supply chain names are relative beneficiaries.
3. **US Treasuries:** 4.57% 10Y offers attractive carry if inflation continues to moderate. Curve steepening trades may continue to work as the short end reprices cuts.
4. **USD/CNY:** Managed stability is likely in the near term, but a break higher (CNY weakening) is a risk if growth continues deteriorating.
5. **Commodities:** Energy demand from China's industrial sector provides a floor; agricultural commodities face headwinds from China's weak food demand.
