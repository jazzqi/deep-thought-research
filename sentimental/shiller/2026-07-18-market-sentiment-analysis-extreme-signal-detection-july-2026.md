# Market Sentiment Analysis: Extreme Signal Detection
## Date: 2026-07-18
## Analyst: shiller

### Overview

This report analyzes current market sentiment using available macro indicators, with a focus on detecting extreme sentiment signals. The analysis covers both China and US markets.

---

### 1. China Market: Multiple Extreme Signals Detected

#### 1.1 Consumer Confidence: 89.9 — DEEP PESSIMISM

**Data**: Consumer Confidence Index at **89.9** (2026年5月, 78天前)
**Interpretation**: This is deeply below the 100 neutral threshold. Historically, consumer confidence below 90 in China has been associated with periods of severe economic stress. The last time China's consumer confidence was in this territory was during the COVID lockdown period and the property crisis of 2022-2023.

**Signal Level**: 🔴 EXTREME (Priority 1)
- Below 100 = contraction in consumer sentiment
- Below 90 = crisis-level pessimism
- At 89.9, this signals households are extremely cautious about their economic prospects, suppressing consumption

#### 1.2 Fixed Asset Investment: -15.6% YoY — COLLAPSE IN BUSINESS CONFIDENCE

**Data**: FAI YoY at **-15.6%** (2026年6月)
**Interpretation**: A decline of this magnitude in fixed asset investment reflects an outright collapse in business investment appetite. Companies are refusing to commit capital to expansion or new projects. This is far beyond a mere slowdown — it is a contraction.

**Signal Level**: 🔴 EXTREME (Priority 1)
- Negative FAI growth is rare in China's growth model
- -15.6% is among the most severe readings on record outside of the initial COVID shock
- Implies extreme risk aversion in the corporate sector

#### 1.3 New Loans: -25.2% YoY — CREDIT CRUNCH IN PROGRESS

**Data**: New Loan YoY at **-25.2%** (2026年6月)
**Interpretation**: A quarter-over-year decline in new lending of this magnitude signals a severe credit contraction. This could be driven by both supply-side (banks tightening) and demand-side (borrowers unwilling to take on debt). Either way, it indicates extreme risk aversion in the financial system.

**Signal Level**: 🔴 EXTREME (Priority 1)
- New loans contracting 25% YoY is a massive credit event
- Suggests both banks and borrowers are in "risk-off" mode
- This will compound the FAI weakness (no credit → no investment)

#### 1.4 Retail Sales: +1.0% YoY — CONSUMPTION STAGNATION

**Data**: Retail Sales YoY at **1.0%** (2026年6月)
**Interpretation**: Retail sales growing at only 1% in nominal terms means real consumption may be negative when adjusted for actual price levels. This is consistent with the consumer confidence data — households are not spending.

**Signal Level**: 🟡 ELEVATED CONCERN
- Well below pre-pandemic trend of 8-10%
- In real terms, likely zero or negative
- Reflects the consumer confidence collapse

#### 1.5 Bond Yield: 10Y at 1.74% — EXTREME SAFE-HAVEN FLOWS

**Data**: China 10Y Government Bond Yield at **1.7404%** (2026-07-17)
**Interpretation**: China 10Y yields at 1.74% are at the lower end of historical ranges. This indicates massive safe-haven demand for Chinese government bonds, consistent with extreme risk aversion in equity and credit markets.

**Signal Level**: 🟡 NOTABLE
- Low yields reflect flight-to-safety
- Yield spread 10Y-2Y at 0.48bp — curve is positive but flat
- SHIBOR 3M at 1.43% — ample liquidity but not flowing to risk assets

#### 1.6 Inflation Divergence: Energy Up 17.7%, Agriculture Down 4.9%

**Data**: CGPI Energy YoY at **+17.74%**, CGPI Agricultural YoY at **-4.94%** (2026年6月)
**Interpretation**: This is a massive divergence. Energy costs are soaring while agricultural prices are deflating. This creates a "cost-push" pressure on industrial sectors that rely on energy while consumer-side inflation remains absent.

**Signal Level**: 🟡 WATCH
- Energy inflation + Agricultural deflation = asymmetric pressure
- Suggests margin compression for energy-intensive industries
- Consumers get relief on food but face higher energy costs

#### 1.7 GDP: 4.7% — Below 5% Target

**Data**: GDP Quarterly at **4.7%** (2026 H1)
**Interpretation**: China's GDP growth is running below the politically important 5% target. Combined with the other indicators, this suggests policymakers may need to respond with stimulus.

**Signal Level**: 🟡 WATCH

### 4. Narrative Hypothesis

**Dominant Theme: China Stagflationary Contraction**

The data paints a picture of an economy suffering from a synchronous contraction across three critical dimensions:

1. **Consumption** (Consumer Confidence 89.9 + Retail Sales 1.0%): Households are deeply pessimistic and pulling back spending.
2. **Investment** (FAI -15.6%): Businesses are refusing to invest, reflecting extreme uncertainty about the future.
3. **Credit** (New Loans -25.2%): The financial system is in de facto credit crunch mode.

These three forces feed into each other: weak consumption → no incentive to invest → no demand for credit → banks tighten → further weakness in consumption.

The ultra-low bond yield (1.74%) suggests the bond market is pricing in continued weakness and potential easing. But the credit data (-25.2% new loans) suggests that monetary easing alone may not be enough — there is a "pushing on a string" problem where liquidity sits idle.

**Policy Implication**: The data suggests a strong case for fiscal stimulus (direct government spending) rather than relying on monetary policy alone to break the negative feedback loop.

**ESG / Climate Note**: The CGPI divergence (energy +17.7%, agriculture -4.9%) introduces an asymmetric inflation risk that complicates the policy response.

### 6. Conclusion

**Overall Sentiment Assessment: EXTREME PESSIMISM (China) / CAUTIOUS (US)**

The most extreme signals are concentrated in China, where three simultaneous contractions (consumption, investment, credit) have created a potentially self-reinforcing downward cycle. The consumer confidence reading of 89.9 is particularly concerning as it sits at levels historically associated with severe economic stress.

The US shows a much more balanced picture: sticky inflation limits policy flexibility, but the labor market remains healthy and consumer confidence is cautious rather than panicked.

**Key Risk**: The negative feedback loop in China (weak consumption → no investment → no credit → weaker consumption) could deepen if not addressed by forceful fiscal intervention. The bond market's 1.74% yield suggests investors are already pricing in this scenario.

**Watch Points for Next Week**:
1. Any policy announcements from China (fiscal stimulus, RRR cut, property sector support)
2. US earnings season commentary on China exposure
3. USD/CNY stability (currently 6.78 — any sharp move would amplify stress)
